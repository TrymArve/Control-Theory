Consider $t \mapsto f(t)$
Iff:
- $\forall~\varepsilon>0$
	- $\exists~\delta >0 : \forall~(t_1,t_2)$
		- $|t_1 - t_2| < \delta \implies |f(t_1)-f(t_2)| < \varepsilon$
then
- $f$ is uniformly continuous

**Note:** $\delta$ and $\varepsilon$ are independent of $t$.
